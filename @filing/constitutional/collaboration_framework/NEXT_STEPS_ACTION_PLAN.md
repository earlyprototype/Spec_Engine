## WHAT COMES NEXT

### Phase 2: Scene-Level Planning (Estimated: 8-12 sessions)

**Break each chapter into scenes with:**
- Scene number and title
- Who's present
- Where it happens
- What happens (2-3 sentence summary)
- Emotional tone/purpose
- Key beats/moments
- Rough word count target

**Example scene card:**

```
CHAPTER 3, SCENE 2: "The Decision Log"
Location: Maya's apartment, evening
Present: Maya alone (construct references)
Word count target: ~1,200

What happens:
Maya obsessively reviews Professional-Maya's decision logs from the past week. Every decision is technically correct, using her own documented heuristics. She tries to identify what she would've done differently. Can't. Realizes the construct knows her decision-tree better than she consciously tracks it.

Beats:
- Opens logs (routine check becomes obsession)
- Finds decision that surprises her initially
- Reviews her own constitutional rules (construct followed them)
- Tries to find ANY decision she'd make differently
- Can't identify a single one
- Question crystallizes: "If optimization uses my rules, which version is 'me'?"

Emotional tone: Growing unease, intellectual defeat
Purpose: Show construct-substrate blurring (core horror)
Ends: Maya closes laptop, stares at reflection in black screen
```

**Process:**
- I draft scene cards for one chapter (5-7 scenes typically)
- You review, refine, approve
- We move to next chapter
- Can jump around (don't need to be linear)

#### Option C: Real-Time Collaborative

**Workflow:**
1. I write opening paragraph
2. You respond with direction/feedback
3. I adapt immediately
4. Continue in conversation until scene complete

### Phase 5: Self-Revision (Multiple passes)

**Pass 1: Structural Edit**
- Does plot work?
- Pacing right?
- Character arcs satisfying?
- Any plot holes?
- Scenes to cut/add?

**Pass 2: Scene-Level Edit**
- Each scene necessary?
- Dialogue natural?
- Character voices distinct?
- Tension adequate?

**Pass 3: Line Edit**
- Sentence rhythm
- Word choice
- Prose quality
- Cut unnecessary words

**Pass 4: Proofreading**
- Grammar
- Typos
- Consistency

**Your role:** Direct changes, approve/reject
**AI role:** Execute changes, track consistency, present options

**Time:** 2-4 months (can do one pass, break, return)

### Phase 6: Beta Readers (2 months)

**Find 3-5 trusted readers:**
- Mix of writers and avid readers
- Ideally familiar with PKD / psychological sci-fi
- NOT family (too biased)
- Someone with narcissistic parent experience (for mother dynamics)

**Give them:**

- Don't defend your choices
- Thank them
- Implement what serves your vision

## WHAT IF I GET STUCK?

### Common Sticking Points:

**"I've lost excitement for the project"**
- Normal. Take 1-2 week break. Excitement returns.
- Or jump to exciting scene you haven't written yet.

**"This scene isn't working"**
- Skip it. Come back later. Write the next one.
- Or: Write placeholder ("They talk about X, then Y happens") and move on.

**"I can't decide between two options"**
- Draft both (AI can do this fast).
- Read them. Your gut will know.

**"I'm overwhelmed by the scope"**
- Zoom in. Today's goal: Just this one scene.
- One scene at a time becomes a novel.

**"I'm doubting the whole story"**
- Normal. Every writer feels this.
- Finish anyway. Revision fixes doubtful parts.
- Or: Take break, read something inspiring, return.
